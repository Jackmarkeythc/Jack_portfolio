Executive Summary

My work spans course projects (HCI/UX, data‑viz, AR/MR) and a guitar‑culture e‑commerce concept. Reviewers found it hard to navigate and grasp the story quickly. I redesigned the portfolio around an accessible “Select Work” grid of fully clickable, keyboard‑navigable cards that open focused project details (modal or page). I preserved my original typographic voice while tightening contrast, spacing, and theme tokens for light/dark. The result is a cohesive experience that communicates who I am in seconds and lets recruiters deep‑dive fast.

Outcomes (early signals)

  • Clear entry point (“Select Work”) and consistent case‑study structure
  • Faster path‑to‑content (≤2 clicks from landing to any project)
  • Accessible interactions (visible focus, ARIA labels, Escape to close modals)
  • A modular foundation to add a guitar shop demo without fragmenting the brand

Background & Problem

Fragmented story: Projects lived across classes and experiments, with uneven depth and labels.

Interaction gap: The “Select Work” area looked like cards but wasn’t fully clickable/activatable; keyboard support was inconsistent.

Brand vs readability: I prefer keeping original fonts, but some pages had low contrast and tight line‑height, especially in dark mode.

Objectives & Success Metrics

  • Clarity in 10s: Communicate role and value on the hero + nav.
  • Findability: Reach a relevant project in ≤2 clicks.
  • Consistency: Unify case‑study sections (Context → Problem → Process → Solution → Impact).
  • Accessibility: WCAG 2.1 AA color contrast; keyboard‑navigable cards and modals.
  • Performance: Lighthouse ≥90 across Performance/Accessibility/Best Practices/SEO.
  • Engagement: Increase average time‑on‑portfolio and % of visitors who view ≥2 projects.

Research & Inputs

Milanote synthesis: I mapped sections, assets, and the relationship between portfolio and guitar shop concept. Light interviews and quick tests informed the need for predictable card affordances and clear visual rhythm.

Design System & Interaction

Design tokens, accessible focus states, and a small component grammar (Card, Tag, Button, Modal) ensure consistency. Cards are full‑card clickable, keyboard‑activatable (Enter/Space), and show visible focus rings. Modals trap focus and return it to the trigger on close.

Implementation & Process

Stack: HTML/CSS/JS with lightweight components; Bootstrap semantics where useful. Process: Milanote → Figma → Build → Test → Iterate. GA4 events instrumented for card_open, modal_view, and view_case_study.

Solution Highlights

  • Predictable Select Work grid with accessible, fully clickable cards.
  • Standardized case‑study page structure for scannability and deep reads.
  • Light/Dark tokens audited for hover/focus/active states.
  • Shop demo inherits the same component grammar for cohesion.

Evidence & Next Steps

Collected before/after screenshots, Lighthouse & AXE reports, and GA4 event samples. Next: expand the shop demo, add filters, and continue collecting quantitative signals.

What I Learned

Consistency reduces cognitive load; accessible interactions matter; preserving fonts works with tuned rhythm and contrast; theme toggles require state audits.

Deliverables

Case pages, modal interactions, screenshots, Lighthouse/AXE reports, and GA4 instrumentation. Ready to drop into the portfolio and link from the Select Work grid.
